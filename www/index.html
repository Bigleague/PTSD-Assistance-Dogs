PTSD Assistance Dogs

Usually they are Golden Retrievers or Labradors. Assistance dogs will be desexed. The dog will have identification indicating that it is an assistance dog and the disability that it is being , or has been trained , to assist. PTSD Assistance Dogs are placed with people living with post-traumatic stress disorder. PTSD Assistance Dogs are trained to work with the very individual and specfic needs of their owner.

PTSD Assistance
Dogs

A PTSD Assistance Dog can master cues to help their owner overcome psychological trauma linked to specific situations.
• Standing in front of their owner offering a barrier and space.
• Positioning itself behind their owner, to ease hyperawareness, the feeling of being constantly on edge.
• Diverting their owner’s attention to the dog, to bring their owner back to the present moment.
• Providing continuous companionship and a sense of routine. 

Considerations when making the decision of having an Assistance Dog


–Do you Know?
1. What you want the dog to do for you?
There are many tasks a can do, but there are many things they cannot.
2. Are you ready for increased public attention?
You will draw attention to yourself and your disability with an assistance dog.
3. Can you handle confrontation?
Even if your dog is fully accreditated, some people will deny you access to places you have every right to entre.
4. Are you ready for a long-term (ten to fifteen years) committment that comes with Assistance Dog ownership?
5. Do you have the time and patience to work with an assistance dog both initially in the partnership and commit to ongoing training during the dog's life?
6. An assistance dog is a big responsibility:  You will be leagally responsible for the behaviour of your dog at all times.

How to apply for a Dog

PTSD Assistance Dogs must be trained to aliveate your disability. You must get a recommendation and detailed report from your Doctors as to the nature of your disability and explainhow an Assistance Dog can help to alleviate the effect of your disability. (this is required by law) To meet the Disability Discrimination Act’s requirements evidence is required that the Assistance Dog is trained to meet the standards of hygiene and behaviour.

Registering an Assistance Dog

You are required to provide a signed statement or documentation from a recognized training body that your dog is being, or has been trained, as an assistance animal and a signed statement from you and the training body that the animal is being used as an assistance animal.
You register your dog with your local council for the life of the dog.
